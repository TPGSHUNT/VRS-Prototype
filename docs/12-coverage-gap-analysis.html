Dollar General · Vendor Rebate System

Documentation Coverage & Gap Analysis

2026-05-07 · TPG Partners · Estimate of how much of the legacy VRS business functionality is documented well enough to rebuild, and what closes each gap · Revised 2026-05-10 after Ken rounds 1–5

Roughly 68–73% of the application's business functionality is documented to rebuild-grade (up from 55–65% before Ken's responses). Coverage is uneven: the core AP daily flow is in good shape; calculation engine internals and specialized subsystems carry most of the remaining gap. The 8-week prototype timeline does not require closing all of this; production cutover before Ken's January 2027 retirement does. Round 5 surfaced two previously-unknown outbound interfaces (Gold pricing, Treasury invoices) and a load-bearing architectural fact — VRS lives inside the RSL Oracle DB instance — that reshapes the migration story.

The remaining gap shifts toward three centres of mass:
  • CODE Oracle PL/SQL dives for engine internals, the NSA / S5S5 / Damages packages, audit triggers, and the newly-surfaced Gold pricing views — no longer Ken-blocked, just labour-intensive.
  • EXT External DG teams — the 1010 owner group, Oracle ERP / Lawson integration teams (D6), Gold & Treasury consumer teams (D7), vendor-portal ownership. None answerable by Ken.
  • SME Khari for DG-side strategy — modernization roadmap context, federated-read decision, and the strategic flag below.
Round-5 architectural insight: VRS lives inside the RSL Oracle DB instance. What we'd been calling "external integrations" for read-side data is mostly intra-instance cross-schema queries. The rewrite is therefore schema extraction from a shared Oracle instance, not standalone-app replatform. Doc 09 §2 and §3 have been restructured around Ken's interface taxonomy (true outbound interfaces vs. data access). Two outbound interfaces previously absent from architecture docs — Gold (pricing views) and Treasury (invoice table-drop) — are now logged.

Strategic flag (added 2026-05-09): Per Ken, DG's active modernization (Lawson AP → Oracle ERP) is being engineered to preserve VRS unchanged. That implies the rebuild is TPG-driven rather than DG-driven and changes the urgency framing of the pitch. Not in scope of this gap analysis — flagged for David's DG-side conversation with Khari before further commitment to the rebuild architecture.
