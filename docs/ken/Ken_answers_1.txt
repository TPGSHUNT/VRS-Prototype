This best way to think this is how often a Vendor is billed --- how
often the system will Create A Deduction and or Invoice for purposes of
billing  The original Frequencies were:

 

-   Period

-   Quarter

 

Along the way **Weekly** and End of **Rebate** were added.  Weekly is a
very specialize process that creates a deduction per week.  It is
special because VRS works by Period -- Weekly just does not fit in to
that concept.  It was shoe-horned in.   I have literally (with the past
few weeks) added the ability for there to be user defined Frequences but
they all act like End of Rebate.

 

What they do a lot these days for Vendors that are set up as Deduct
(which most are -- a vendor has to have a lot of pull to no be set up
this way)  is the run On Demand Deductions.   This allows then create a
Deduction without waiting for the End of Period processing.

one comes in from the Portal,  and then a Rebate Program is created from
that Agreement, then the Rebate Vendor will have a reference to the
Agreement.  But there are many, many Rebates that have no corresponding
Agreement on the MDSE side.  Pretty much any Rebate created from a Sub
System (NSA, DG Media, BOPIS, Customer Bounce Back etc.) do not have
Agreements on the MDSE Side.  This is one of the reasons FPA (Forecast
and Planning) abandoned using VRS even though they were the ones that
designed the  MDSE side (this is why you do no let Business people do
the design). The Agreements in the MDSE side do not even come close to
representing a complete picture.

** **

**Rebate Flag -- Not Used **(I don't remember what its original purpose
was)

** **

**Both Flag** (Y or NULL) -- used with the Source Receipts -- when set
to Yes indicate to the Extract (the E in the E&C)  to use Both Import
and Domestic Receipts

 

**Core** is a reference to whether not an Item is carried year round.  
DG also has seasonal Items -- these are not Core.  On the Rebate program
for certain sources (Ex: Receipts) , you can indicate whether only Core,
Non Core or Both are used by the Extract (the E in the E&C).  

 

**Delete Flag** -- this just means that the Rebate is marked for
Delete.  It is prohibitive from a performance perspective to delete a
Rebate directly from the Form.  So the Delete Flag was introduced to
mark the Rebate for Delete and then a Nighty process deletes them.  

 

**SKU Only Flag** (Y or NULL)  - This a form level control. When set to
Yes it does not allow you to use the Exclude SKU feature.
