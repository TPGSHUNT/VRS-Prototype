RE: A little follow-up  (Ken, 2026-05-12 13:06; replying to David's 6 questions)
================================================================================

#1 — The setting on Tobacco is intentional.
[Context: analyst Tobacco access being read-only is by design, not a bug.]

#2 — Here is the Hierarchy for the AP Dept in terms of diminishing access:
     VRS_ADMIN -> VRS_MANAGER -> VRS_SUPERVISOPR -> VRS_APP_USER
     It's not a formal hierarchy in the sense that you can determine it using
     just the data, but this is how it is used. Note that almost everyone does
     the job of an analyst but some have more authority in the system. In terms
     of your chart, the first 3 fall under the Manager (your usage) role.

#3 — VRS_MKTING, VRS_PRICING as well as VRS_DAMAGES allow limited access to
     certain pieces within VRS. These are used by departments other than AP or
     MDSE. Pricing uses the MDSE side. They create TPR Agreements and pull
     Prices out of VRS for the TPR Agreements. Marketing has access to the AD
     Coop Subsystem on the MDSE Side (1 person uses this). Damages is a new
     Group and opens up access to one button on one of the Damages forms (1
     person uses this).

#4 — MDSE_VENDOR_CONTACTS is a reference to a set of tables and a Form that
     maintenances them. These contain both the Vendor and the List of Portal
     User IDs and is used by the Portal to back into the Vendor using Userid
     used to log in. The Security Dept has access to this form via this Group.

#5 — FPA (Forecast and planning) — the head of that Dept at the time is who
     designed the MDSE side. They originally were supposed to oversee and be
     the experts and administrators on the MDSE side. The woman that designed
     it left the company the week it went live. Less than two years later the
     FPA Dept abandoned the system but is stuck supporting it. So now, generally
     there is usually at least one person assigned as FPA_SUPERVIOR in their
     group to support and Administer the MDSE side.
     [Resolves the handoff's "FPA-encoded-vs-operating" fork: design per current
      use, not the original FPA spec. The MDSE side has no real owner.]

#6 — None. Only Buyers and their delegates have access to the Buyers Dashboard.
     If AP has access to this information it would be through some report either
     internal (Oracle Report) or external (Web Focus).

Interpretation (consistent with Ken round 3): an Agreement whose Forecast
exceeds $250,000 routes to Pending DMM Approval; exceeding $1,000,000 then
routes to Pending GMM Approval after DMM. DMM-approval-of-all-TPRs is currently
disabled (DMM_APPROVE_TPR = No). Check-paying vendors not on the exclusion
Parameter list also force DMM approval. These thresholds were previously
unknown / synthesized — this resolves docs/05 item A.5.
