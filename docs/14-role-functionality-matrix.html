Role × Functionality Matrix

2026-05-12 · TPG Partners · Revised post-Ken feedback (May 11) + reconciled against DG Roles.xlsx

This matrix describes the new VRS landing-page IA in terms of what each role does and sees. It is now reconciled against two ground-truth inputs: Ken's May 11 walkthrough of the matrix and DG Roles.xlsx (the production export of the 15 VRS security groups across 90 forms, captured locally as docs/ken/DG_Roles_flat.csv). The business-role columns below are still the design lens — they map to one or more VRS security groups, summarized in the next section.

1. Security groups — ground truth from DG Roles.xlsx

VRS has 15 security groups, each AP-side (A) or MDSE-side (M). Membership is per-user via SSO → VRS_SECURITY mapping; each group has Maintenance / Read-only / None at the form level. The seven business-role columns in the matrix map to these groups as follows:

Business role VRS group(s) Side Notes
APA — AP Analyst VRS_APP_USER AP 45 M / 45 R forms. Routine working role on the AP side. Cannot do Review / Approve / Finalize at period close.
APM — AP Manager VRS_MANAGER AP 59 M / 31 R. Newer role. Near-VRS_ADMIN minus three forms: MAINTENANCE.SECURITY, MAINTENANCE.REBATE_TYPES, MAINTENANCE.ACCOUNTING_CONTROLS.
BYR/DEL — Buyer or Delegate VRS_MDSE_BUYER, VRS_MDSE_USER MDSE 24 M / 5 R. Delegates are proxies for a Buyer (FPA is delegated for all buyers in practice) — same access set. Ken-fix
FPA — Forecast & Planning VRS_FPA_SUPERVISOR, VRS_FPA_USER MDSE Supervisor: 36 M / 3 R (highest MDSE authority — owns Cycle Close, POG, Forecast ID, MDSE Rebate Types setup). User: 27 M / 8 R. added
DMM VRS_MDSE_DMM MDSE 22 M / 6 R. Identical form-level access to GMM/SVP (see below).
GMM/SVP VRS_MDSE_SVP MDSE 22 M / 6 R. Identical at the form level to DMM — the Buyer→DMM→GMM/SVP hierarchy is enforced in Move Forward / status-routing business logic, not in form-level security. The matrix shouldn't imply broader form access. data-ground-truth
R/O — Read-only VRS_ADMIN_RO AP 4 M / 86 R. Finance audit / exec dashboard scope. Almost everything visible, almost nothing writable.
Five groups are not yet bridged to a business-role column — they're either niche or need Ken's clarification before being modeled in the IA:
  • VRS_ADMIN — 62 M / 28 R. Owns MAINTENANCE.SECURITY exclusively. Treat as superuser; not a routine business role.
  • VRS_SUPERVISOR — 50 M / 39 R, AP-side. Sits between APA and APM in privilege. Tobacco-accrual edit access starts here. Where does this fit operationally?
  • VRS_DAMAGES — 2 M / 87 R / 1 N, AP-side. Narrow Damages-only operator role.
  • VRS_MKTING, VRS_PRICING — 20 M each, MDSE-side. Domain-specific; purpose unclear.
  • VRS_MDSE_VENDOR_CONTACTS — 88 N / 2 M. Quirk: tagged AP user-type despite MDSE prefix. Effectively a single-purpose role.

3. Out of scope of this matrix

  • Vendor Portal users are Vendors, not DG associates — though DG controls portal logins. They live in the separate Java/Tomcat app; their action set (view own programs, submit agreements, view 1010-derived pace-to-target) is described in 09-current-architecture.html §2 and unchanged by this matrix.
  • Service accounts: Calculate, Batch-Exec, S5S5_Calculate, QUEUE, Upload, Finalize — scheduled-work identities, no UI session.
  • NSA & S5S5 subsystem operators: Ken confirmed these subsystems are handled by specific associates in practice but there's no dedicated security group for them — all associates have access. The Tobacco subsystem is the only one with a hard role restriction.

4. Open questions for Ken (revised)

Most of the original questions have now been answered by the May 11 walkthrough + DG Roles.xlsx. What remains:

  • Tobacco read-only for analysts. SUBSYSTEMS.TOBACCO_ACCRUAL is M for VRS_ADMIN, VRS_MANAGER, VRS_SUPERVISOR and R for VRS_APP_USER and VRS_DAMAGES. Is that R-access intentional, or should APA see N per your "VRS_SUPERVISOR and above" policy intent?

  • VRS_MKTING and VRS_PRICING. MDSE-side, 20 M each. What are they used for? Should they have their own column in this matrix, or fold under something else?
  • VRS_MDSE_VENDOR_CONTACTS quirk. Tagged as AP user_type despite the MDSE prefix; near-empty access (88 N / 2 M). Intentional, or a definitional accident?
  • FPA-encoded vs FPA-operating. The system encodes FPA Supervisor as the most-privileged MDSE role (Cycle Close, POG, Forecast IDs, MDSE Rebate Types, DG Racing, Consolidate Vendors). You've said the FPA team has stepped back from operating VRS in practice. For the rebuild, do we honor the encoded model or design around the operating reality (e.g. shift FPA-Supervisor's authorities to Buyer leads or a new role)?
  • "Renewals approaching" visibility for AP. You flagged that AP doesn't see Buyer-Dashboard renewal alerts today but probably should. Confirm we should treat this as an enhancement target in the new system?
Answered — closed: SVP role (real, identical to DMM at form level); AP-stage approval (APA primary, not APM); Read-only scope (full vendor portfolio, no division split); APA reassignment (no such concept on AP side); on-demand deduction (APA + APM only); APM in approval queue (handled via the APM column on AP-stage rows); 1010 in-app analytics for all roles (universal — ad-hoc 1010 queries outside VRS are also open to all analysts).
